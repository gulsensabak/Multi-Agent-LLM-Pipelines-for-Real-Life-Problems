**Germany:**

1. **Otto von Bismarck (1815–1898)**
   - Known as the "Iron Chancellor," Bismarck was responsible for the unification of Germany in 1871.
   - He implemented Realpolitik, a pragmatic approach to politics.
   - Introduced the first modern welfare state with social security and healthcare reforms.
   - Played a crucial role in European diplomacy, keeping Germany at peace for two decades.

2. **Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (1749–1832)**
   - A literary giant, Goethe was a poet, playwright, novelist, and scientist.
   - His most famous work, *Faust*, is considered one of the greatest pieces of German literature.
   - His contributions to the Sturm und Drang movement and German classicism were significant.

3. **Albert Einstein (1879–1955)**
   - Developed the theory of relativity, fundamentally changing our understanding of physics.
   - Received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the photoelectric effect.
   - Played a key role in the development of quantum mechanics and modern cosmology.

---

**Japan:**

1. **Tokugawa Ieyasu (1543–1616)**
   - Founder of the Tokugawa Shogunate, ruling Japan for over 250 years.
   - Unified Japan after the Battle of Sekigahara in 1600.
   - Implemented policies of isolationism, ensuring stability but limiting foreign influence.

2. **Emperor Meiji (1852–1912)**
   - Oversaw the Meiji Restoration, modernizing Japan politically, economically, and militarily.
   - Led Japan’s transformation from a feudal society to an industrialized world power.

3. **Oda Nobunaga (1534–1582)**
   - One of the most influential warlords of the Sengoku period.
   - Pioneered the use of firearms in battle and revolutionized military tactics.
   - Laid the foundation for Japan's unification, later completed by his successors.

---

**United States:**

1. **George Washington (1732–1799)**
   - First President of the United States and leader of the Continental Army during the American Revolution.
   - Set many precedents for the U.S. presidency, including the two-term tradition.
   - Advocated for national unity and neutrality in foreign affairs.

2. **Abraham Lincoln (1809–1865)**
   - Led the U.S. through the Civil War, preserving the Union and abolishing slavery.
   - Delivered the Gettysburg Address, one of the most famous speeches in American history.
   - Signed the Emancipation Proclamation, which freed enslaved people in Confederate states.

3. **Martin Luther King Jr. (1929–1968)**
   - Key leader in the American Civil Rights Movement.
   - Advocated for nonviolent resistance and delivered the iconic "I Have a Dream" speech.
   - Played a crucial role in the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965.

---

**Turkey:**

1. **Mustafa Kemal Atatürk (1881–1938)**
   - Founder of the Republic of Turkey and its first President.
   - Led the Turkish War of Independence and abolished the Ottoman Sultanate.
   - Implemented widespread reforms in law, education, language, and women's rights.
   - Established a secular, modern state, transforming Turkey into a progressive nation.

2. **Suleiman the Magnificent (1494–1566)**
   - Longest-reigning Sultan of the Ottoman Empire (1520–1566).
   - Expanded the empire to its greatest territorial extent, reaching Europe, Africa, and the Middle East.
   - Patron of the arts and law, overseeing a golden age in Ottoman architecture and literature.

3. **Rumi (1207–1273)**
   - A Persian poet and Sufi mystic, deeply influential in Islamic philosophy and spirituality.
   - His poetry emphasizes love, tolerance, and unity, inspiring people across cultures.
   - Founder of the Mevlevi Order, known for the Whirling Dervishes.

# Historical Figures by Country

## France

1. **Napoleon Bonaparte (1769–1821)**
   - Military leader and Emperor of France who dominated European affairs for over a decade.
   - Introduced the Napoleonic Code, which influenced legal systems worldwide.
   - Expanded French territory but ultimately was defeated at Waterloo.

2. **Louis XIV (1638–1715)**
   - Known as the "Sun King," he ruled France for 72 years, making it a dominant European power.
   - Built the Palace of Versailles and centralized royal authority.
   - His reign saw the peak of French cultural and military influence.

3. **Joan of Arc (1412–1431)**
   - A peasant girl who led French forces to victory against the English during the Hundred Years' War.
   - Claimed to have received visions from God instructing her to support Charles VII.
   - Captured by the English and executed for heresy, later canonized as a saint.

## United Kingdom

1. **Winston Churchill (1874–1965)**
   - Prime Minister of the UK during World War II, known for his defiant speeches against Nazi Germany.
   - Played a key role in forming the Allied strategy and post-war reconstruction.
   - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature for his historical writings.

2. **Queen Elizabeth I (1533–1603)**
   - Ruled England during the Elizabethan Era, a period of cultural flourishing.
   - Defeated the Spanish Armada, establishing England as a global naval power.
   - Patron of the arts, supporting figures like William Shakespeare.

3. **Isaac Newton (1643–1727)**
   - Physicist and mathematician, formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation.
   - Made significant contributions to calculus and optics.
   - His work laid the foundation for classical mechanics.

## China

1. **Confucius (551–479 BCE)**
   - Philosopher and teacher whose ideas on morality, government, and ethics shaped Chinese civilization.
   - Founded Confucianism, which influenced East Asian cultures for centuries.
   - His teachings emphasized respect, social harmony, and proper conduct.

2. **Qin Shi Huang (259–210 BCE)**
   - First Emperor of China, unifying warring states into one empire.
   - Ordered the construction of the Great Wall and standardized Chinese script, currency, and measurements.
   - Known for his autocratic rule and the famous Terracotta Army in his tomb.

3. **Mao Zedong (1893–1976)**
   - Founder of the People's Republic of China and leader of the Communist Party.
   - Led the Chinese Revolution and introduced policies like the Great Leap Forward and the Cultural Revolution.
   - His rule transformed China into a socialist state but also caused significant upheavals.

## India

1. **Mahatma Gandhi (1869–1948)**
   - Leader of India's non-violent independence movement against British rule.
   - Advocated for civil disobedience and passive resistance.
   - Assassinated in 1948, his philosophy of nonviolence influenced global movements.

2. **Ashoka the Great (304–232 BCE)**
   - Emperor of the Maurya Dynasty who expanded his empire through military conquest.
   - Later adopted Buddhism and promoted peace, moral governance, and religious tolerance.
   - His edicts on pillars across India emphasized dharma (righteousness).

3. **Rabindranath Tagore (1861–1941)**
   - Poet, philosopher, and Nobel Laureate in Literature (1913).
   - Wrote India's national anthem, *Jana Gana Mana*, and made significant contributions to education and cultural revival.
   - His literary works influenced Indian nationalism and global literature.

## Spain

1. **Isabella I of Castile (1451–1504)**
   - United Spain through her marriage to Ferdinand II and completed the Reconquista.
   - Sponsored Christopher Columbus’s voyage, leading to European exploration of the Americas.
   - Reformed Spain’s government and strengthened Catholic influence.

2. **Francisco Franco (1892–1975)**
   - Led Nationalist forces to victory in the Spanish Civil War and ruled Spain as a dictator for nearly four decades.
   - Maintained Spanish neutrality in World War II but aligned with anti-communist policies.
   - Suppressed political opposition and centralized power.

3. **Pablo Picasso (1881–1973)**
   - One of the most influential artists of the 20th century, co-founder of Cubism.
   - His work *Guernica* depicted the horrors of war and became an anti-war symbol.
   - Prolific in various artistic mediums, including painting, sculpture, and printmaking.
